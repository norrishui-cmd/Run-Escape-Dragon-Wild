## Strategic position

The official Dragonwilds Wiki is the strongest database competitor. This site should not try to win with copied definitions. Its opportunity is player-problem SEO: direct answers, progression routes, quest stuck points, safe farming loops, update impact pages, server/platform troubleshooting, and comparisons that connect several database facts into an action.

The 2,000 URL target is an inventory ceiling, not permission to index thin templates. A candidate URL enters the sitemap only after it has a concrete answer, verified facts, unique intent, useful internal links and a current-patch label.

## Delivery phases

### Phase 1 — foundation and current-demand pages (completed in this package)

- Repair stale release, roadmap and platform facts.
- Establish one canonical trailing-slash URL format and a clean sitemap.
- Add automated checks for title, description, H1, canonical, duplicates, broken internal links, structured data and thin content warnings.
- Publish the first verified pages for release date, consoles, Switch 2, Umbral Sands, Fuzan, Fight Cave, Nightmare Crucible, hazards, current materials, crafting stations, servers and Water Magic.
- Strengthen thin legacy hubs with current facts and links into the new cluster.

Exit target: 38 canonical sitemap URLs, zero hard audit failures, all newly created pages fact-sourced and internally linked.

### Phase 2 — verified database seed (target 250–350 URLs)

- Build structured data files for resources, recipes, equipment, enemies, spells and quests.
- First priority: Umbral Sands and Dowdun Reach entities with active post-update demand.
- Publish only complete rows; hold incomplete candidates outside the sitemap.
- Add HTML tables and entity cross-links: source → ingredient → recipe → equipment → activity.

### Phase 3 — task and location depth (target 750–900 URLs)

- Add full quest walkthroughs and one URL per genuine stuck point when the answer differs materially.
- Build region/landmark/resource routes with original screenshots or verified map references.
- Add boss weaknesses, drops and solo/co-op strategies based on current-build testing.
- Use GSC query-to-page mapping to merge cannibalizing pages.

### Phase 4 — full-release preparation (target 1,400–1,600 URLs)

- Prebuild non-indexed Scorned Wilderness templates and publish only after facts are verified.
- Expand PS5, Xbox and Switch 2 troubleshooting once official platform details are available.
- Add update migration pages for changed recipes, stations, stats and progression gates.
- Request crawl for hubs and top-demand leaves, not every low-value URL manually.

### Phase 5 — quality-approved scale (target 2,000–2,050 URLs)

- Fill proven clusters from live queries, community questions and missing entity relationships.
- Run semantic spot checks on every batch; pause any template with weak indexation or engagement.
- Keep sitemap limited to canonical, useful and current pages.
- Consolidate pages with overlapping intent and redirect retired slugs.

## Publishing cadence to September 15

Use a maximum pace of 30–40 candidates per day, but publish fewer when evidence is incomplete. Every daily batch should include automated validation plus manual review of at least the highest-risk pages. Review sitemap discovery and indexing weekly; compare GSC 7-day and 28-day query/page data after sufficient traffic exists.

## Quality gate

A page must have a direct answer in the first screen, verified current-patch facts, distinct title/meta/H1/canonical, at least two useful internal links, a relevant hub, source/provenance, and no placeholder promises. Word count alone never qualifies a page. Pages with unknown quantities, locations, stats or quest steps remain candidates and stay out of the sitemap.
